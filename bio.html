The Story Behind the Work

Hi, I'm Tymofii "Tima" Pidlisnyi ‚Äî though most people just call me Tima. I might look cocky in photos, but I promise I'm just a sweetheart who happened to grow up surrounded by brilliant nerds, and it shaped everything about how I see the world.

Where It All Started

I was born and raised in Kyiv, Ukraine, a city where resilience isn't just a buzzword ‚Äî it's a way of life. I grew up in a world where innovation had to happen despite obstacles, not because everything was perfect. That scrappy, figure-it-out mentality became part of my DNA.

My first love was film directing. I studied at Kyiv National I. K. Karpenko-Kary Theatre, Cinema and Television University, where I learned to tell stories visually, emotionally, and authentically. Film taught me something critical: how to see the invisible structure beneath what appears on the surface. Every frame is a design decision. Every cut is a psychological choice. That lens never left me, even when I moved into tech and product.

The Shift to Entrepreneurship

While still in Ukraine, I founded Amplifier Entertainment Agency, organizing conferences for organizations like UNICEF, USAID, and the ICRC, plus major entertainment events. It was my first taste of building something from scratch, of bringing people together around a vision.

I also worked at Legrem Law Firm as a Strategic Communications & PR Associate, where I mediated business disputes and managed corporate communications during high-stakes transitions ‚Äî including bank seizures. It was intense, political, and taught me how to navigate chaos with clarity.

Moving to America

In 2016, I moved to the United States with $300 in my pocket and zero connections. I landed in New York City and joined Allset, a food-tech startup, as a commission-only sales rep. Within 8 months, I was promoted to VP of Sales. I helped scale the platform from 300 to 3,400+ locations and enabled a $5M Series A. It was the first time I realized: I don't just build things ‚Äî I scale them.

"Success isn't about having advantages. It's about refusing to let their absence stop you."

Building, Failing, Learning, Repeating

After Allset (which was later acquired by SoundHound AI), I joined HAY! Straws as Head of Growth & Partnerships. I secured 2,000+ B2B accounts ‚Äî Hilton, Hyatt, Marriott ‚Äî and generated $4M+ in sales. Sustainability wasn't just a trend for me; it was personal.

That led me to co-found ofNature, an eco CPG brand that created biodegradable acetate-cellulose disposables. We raised $150K pre-seed, built manufacturing in Ukraine, and landed a deal with McDonald's. It was a perfect storm of product innovation, supply chain mastery, and mission-driven branding. And then, like many startups, we hit a wall. We couldn't scale fast enough. But I learned more from that failure than I ever could have from a smooth exit.

Now: Evatar.ai and Beyond

In May 2024, I launched Evatar.ai, an agentic GenAI platform that runs social media on autopilot. In 16 weeks, we shipped an MVP, acquired 121 users, and hit 400K+ weekly impressions. What Drives Me

I'm not building products to chase trends or make a quick exit. I'm building because I believe technology should amplify human creativity, not replace it. I believe the best products come from people who care deeply about craft, context, and authenticity.

I've been called intense. I've been called relentless. And yeah, sometimes I look cocky in photos. But underneath it all, I'm just someone who grew up around brilliant, weird, passionate people who taught me that the world gets better when you build things that didn't exist before.

"Life's too short to build boring stuff or pretend to be someone you're not. Let's make something real."
